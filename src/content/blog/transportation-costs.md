Transportation represents a significant portion of travel budgets in Indonesia. Understanding costs and payment methods for each option helps travelers plan effectively and choose appropriate transport for their needs.

## Ride-Hailing Apps (Grab and Gojek)

**Service Coverage:**
Grab and Gojek operate in major cities including Jakarta, Bali (Denpasar, Ubud, southern areas), Surabaya, Yogyakarta, Bandung, and Medan. Limited or no service in remote areas.

**Pricing Structure:**
- Base fare plus per-kilometer rate
- Surge pricing during peak hours and rain
- Tolls added separately when applicable

**Typical Costs (as of early 2026):**

| Distance | Price (IDR) | Price (USD) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Short ride (2–3 km) | Rp25.000–40.000 | $1.50–$2.40 |
| Medium ride (5–10 km) | Rp50.000–90.000 | $3.00–$5.40 |
| Long ride (20+ km) | Rp150.000–350.000 | $9.00–$21.00 |
| Airport to central area | Rp80.000–250.000 | $4.80–$15.00 |

**Vehicle Options:**

| Option | Description | Price Range |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| GoRide/GrabBike | Motorcycle taxi | Rp10.000–25.000 (short trips) |
| GoCar/GrabCar | Standard car | Standard rate |
| GoCar Plus/GrabCar Plus | Larger vehicles, premium | 30–50% above standard |

**Payment Methods:**
- Cash (always accepted)
- GoPay/OVO credit (in-app)
- Credit/debit card (linked to app)

**Tips for Cost Savings:**
- Walk away from tourist hotspots before booking (avoid surge pricing)
- Book slightly outside peak hours when possible
- Compare Grab and Gojek—prices vary
- Use GoRide/GrabBike for solo travel when comfortable

## Traditional Taxis

**Availability:**
Blue Bird and Express are reputable taxi companies operating in major cities. Street taxis available at airports, hotels, and busy areas.

**Pricing:**

| Distance | Estimated Cost (IDR) |
|----------|----------------------|
| Flag fall | Rp7.000–10.000 |
| Per km | Rp4.000–6.000 |
| Short trip (3 km) | Rp30.000–50.000 |
| Medium trip (8 km) | Rp60.000–100.000 |
| Airport to city center | Rp100.000–300.000 |

**Payment:**
- Cash preferred
- Some accept cards (ask before starting)
- Blue Bird taxis generally metered and reliable

**Negotiated Fares:**
In areas without metered taxis, negotiate price before departure. Expect to pay slightly more than app rates.

## Rental Cars with Driver

**When to Consider:**
- Day trips to multiple locations
- Groups of 3–4 people
- Remote areas without app coverage
- Comfort and convenience priority

**Daily Rates (8–10 hours):**

| Vehicle Type | Price (IDR) | Price (USD) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Small car (Avanza/Xenia) | Rp600.000–900.000 | $36–$54 |
| Medium car (Innova) | Rp800.000–1.200.000 | $48–$72 |

**Included:** Driver, fuel, parking fees

**Not included:** Driver meals (Rp50.000–80.000), tolls, entrance fees

**Payment:**
- Cash usually required
- Partial payment upfront common for multi-day bookings
- Receipt provided upon request

**Arranging:**
- Hotel concierge
- Local travel agencies
- Online platforms (Klook, Traveloka)
- Direct negotiation with drivers

## Self-Drive Car Rental

**Requirements:**
- International Driving Permit (IDP) recommended
- Home country license
- Passport
- Credit card for deposit

**Daily Rates:**

| Vehicle Type | Price (IDR) | Price (USD) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Economy car | Rp300.000–500.000 | $18–$30 |
| Medium car | Rp500.000–800.000 | $30–$48 |
| SUV | Rp800.000–1.500.000 | $48–$90 |

**Excludes:** Fuel (Rp10.000–15.000 per km), insurance, parking

**Considerations:**
- Traffic in major cities is challenging
- Parking can be difficult and expensive
- Navigation requires local knowledge or reliable GPS
- Left-hand traffic

## Scooter/Motorcycle Rental

**Popularity:**
Most common transport for tourists in Bali, Lombok, and beach areas.

**Daily Rates:**

| Scooter Type | Price (IDR) | Price (USD) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Automatic scooter (125cc) | Rp70.000–150.000 | $4.20–$9.00 |
| Semi-automatic | Rp50.000–80.000 | $3.00–$4.80 |
| Weekly rates | 5–7x daily rate | - |

**Requirements:**
- International Driving Permit with motorcycle endorsement
- Home country motorcycle license
- Passport (held as deposit by some renters)
- Deposit: Rp500.000–1.000.000 or passport copy

**Fuel Costs:**

| Item | Cost (IDR) |
|------|------------|
| Pertalite (90 octane) | Rp10.000 per liter |
| Full tank (4–6 liters) | Rp40.000–60.000 |
| Daily usage | Rp20.000–50.000 |

**Safety Notes:**
- Helmet use mandatory and essential
- Road conditions variable
- Insurance rarely covers scooter accidents
- Experience required—Indonesia is not a place to learn

## Public Transportation

**Buses:**
- **TransJakarta (Jakarta):** BRT system, Rp3.500 per trip
- **City buses:** Rp5.000–10.000 depending on distance
- **Intercity buses:** Rp50.000–300.000 depending on route and class

**Trains:**
- **Commuter lines (Jakarta):** Rp3.000–10.000
- **Intercity economy:** Rp50.000–150.000
- **Intercity executive:** Rp200.000–600.000
- **Bookings:** KAI Access app or stations

**Payment:**
- Cash at counters
- Some accept e-money cards
- Apps for advance booking

**Practical Notes:**
- Public transport limited outside major cities
- Can be crowded during peak hours
- English signage minimal on buses
- Trains more comfortable for intercity travel

## Ferries and Boats

**Inter-Island Ferries:**
- Java to Bali: Rp25.000–50.000 (passenger), Rp150.000–300.000 (with vehicle)
- Bali to Lombok: Rp50.000–150.000 depending on class
- Longer routes: Check Pelni for schedules

**Fast Boats (Bali to Gili/Nusa Penida):**
- One-way: Rp250.000–600.000 ($15–$36)
- Round-trip often discounted
- Book through agents or online

**Local Boats:**
- Short crossings: Rp10.000–50.000
- Charter boats: Negotiable, Rp500.000+ per day

## Airport Transfers

**Official Airport Taxis:**
- Pre-paid counters at arrivals
- Fixed rates to major destinations
- Jakarta CGK to center: Rp150.000–300.000
- Bali DPS to Ubud: Rp250.000–350.000
- Bali DPS to Seminyak: Rp150.000–200.000

**Ride-Hailing from Airports:**
- Grab/Gojek have designated pickup points
- Slightly cheaper than official taxis
- May require walking to pickup zone

**Hotel Transfers:**
- Often most expensive option
- Convenience of greeting service
- Typical rates 30–50% above standard taxi

## Regional Cost Variations

**Bali:**
- Ride-hailing: Standard rates
- Private drivers: Rp600.000–800.000/day
- Scooter rental: Rp70.000–150.000/day

**Jakarta:**
- Ride-hailing: Higher due to traffic/tolls
- Tolls: Rp10.000–25.000 per toll road
- Parking: Rp5.000–20.000 per hour in central areas

**Yogyakarta:**
- Andong (horse carts): Rp50.000–100.000/hour
- Becak (cycle rickshaws): Rp20.000–50.000 per trip
- Generally lower transport costs than Bali/Jakarta

**Lombok:**
- Similar to Bali pricing
- Less ride-hailing coverage
- Private drivers: Rp600.000–900.000/day

## Payment Methods by Transport Type

**Cash Required:**
- Traditional taxis (most)
- Buses
- Scooter rentals
- Some private drivers
- Ferries (small operators)

**Cash Preferred:**
- Ride-hailing (though apps accept cards)
- Becak and local transport
- Small rental operations

**Cards Accepted:**
- Major car rental companies
- Some ride-hailing (via app)
- Online bookings
- Airport official taxis (sometimes)

## Budget Planning Guidelines

**Daily Transport Budgets:**
- Budget traveler (scooter/walking + occasional GoCar): Rp50.000–100.000
- Mid-range (mix of GoCar, some private driver): Rp150.000–300.000
- Comfortable (private driver, taxis): Rp500.000–1.000.000

**Weekly Transport Budgets:**
- Budget: Rp350.000–700.000
- Mid-range: Rp1.000.000–2.000.000
- Comfortable: Rp3.500.000–7.000.000

**Special Trip Considerations:**
- Add Rp100.000–200.000 for airport transfers (each way)
- Budget Rp600.000–1.000.000 for day trips with driver
- Inter-island ferries: Rp50.000–600.000 depending on route

## Key Takeaways

1. **Grab/Gojek offer predictable pricing** in covered areas—use as baseline
2. **Scooter rental is cheapest** but requires experience and carries risks
3. **Private drivers cost Rp600.000–1.200.000/day** including fuel
4. **Cash is king for transport** outside ride-hailing apps
5. **Bali and Jakarta have highest transport costs**; other areas cheaper
6. **Airport transfers** cost Rp150.000–350.000 depending on destination
7. **Public transport exists** but limited for tourists outside major cities
8. **Book intercity trains in advance** during peak periods
9. **Always negotiate** un-metered taxi fares before departure
10. **Factor in fuel costs** when comparing self-drive vs. with-driver options
