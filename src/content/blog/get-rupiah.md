Foreign travelers have several options for obtaining Indonesian rupiah. Each method has distinct advantages, costs, and practical considerations. This guide compares the main approaches to help you choose based on your circumstances.

## Bringing Cash and Exchanging Locally

**Foreign Currency Cash Exchange:**
Exchanging USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, SGD, and other major currencies is straightforward at authorized money changers throughout Indonesia.

**Best Currencies to Bring:**
- US Dollars: Universally accepted, best rates
- Euros: Widely accepted, good rates
- British Pounds: Accepted at most changers
- Australian Dollars: Good rates, especially in Bali
- Singapore Dollars: Accepted, particularly in Sumatra and Batam

**Exchange Rate Ranges (February 2026):**

| Currency | Exchange Rate (IDR) |
|----------|---------------------|
| USD | Rp16.400–16.600 per $1 |
| EUR | Rp17.200–17.500 per €1 |
| GBP | Rp20.800–21.200 per £1 |
| AUD | Rp10.800–11.000 per AU$1 |

**Where to Exchange:**
- Authorized money changers: Best rates, safest
- Banks: Good rates, longer hours than changers
- Hotels: Convenient but rates 5–10% worse
- Airport exchanges: Convenience premium of 10–15%

## ATM Withdrawals

**Using International Debit/Credit Cards:**
ATMs are widely available in cities and tourist areas. Most accept Visa, Mastercard, and Cirrus/Maestro networks.

**Bank ATM Fees for Foreign Cards:**

| Bank | Local Fee | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| BCA (Bank Central Asia) | No fee | Best option for tourists |
| Bank Mandiri | No fee | Widely available |
| BNI (Bank Negara Indonesia) | No fee | Reliable network |
| CIMB Niaga | Rp75.000 (~$4.50) | Avoid if possible |
| BRI | Rp30.000 (~$1.80) | Acceptable if no other option |
| Permata Bank | Rp65.000 (~$3.90) | Higher fee alternative |

**Withdrawal Limits:**
- Typical daily limit: Rp10.000.000–25.000.000 ($600–$1.500)
- Per-transaction maximum: Rp2.500.000–5.000.000 ($150–$300)
- Multiple transactions possible up to daily limit

**Exchange Rates at ATMs:**
Banks use interbank rates close to mid-market rates, typically within 1–2% of published rates. Your home bank may add foreign transaction fees (1–3%).

## Pre-Paid Travel Cards

**Multi-Currency Cards (Wise, Revolut, etc.):**
These cards allow you to hold multiple currencies and convert at interbank rates.

**Advantages:**
- Better exchange rates than most banks
- Low or no foreign transaction fees
- Can be topped up remotely
- Transaction tracking via apps

**Considerations:**
- Still subject to ATM operator fees (choose BCA/Mandiri/BNI)
- Internet required for some app functions
- Not all Indonesian ATMs accept all prepaid cards
- Card delivery required before travel

**Indonesian Rupiah Prepaid Cards:**
Limited availability for foreigners. Generally not practical for short-term visitors.

## Credit Card Cash Advances

**Using Credit Cards at ATMs:**
Possible but generally not recommended due to fees.

**Costs:**
- Cash advance fee: 3–5% of amount
- Interest charges: Often immediate, no grace period
- ATM operator fees: Rp0–75.000 depending on bank
- Foreign transaction fees: 1–3% from card issuer

**When to Consider:**
- Emergency situations only
- When debit cards are unavailable
- Short-term bridge until other funds accessible

## Digital Transfer Services

**Western Union and MoneyGram:**
Available for sending money to yourself or receiving from others.

**Process:**
1. Sender initiates transfer online or at agent location
2. Recipient collects cash at Indonesian agent location with ID and reference number
3. Available at post offices, banks, and dedicated outlets

**Costs:**
- Transfer fees: $5–50 depending on amount and urgency
- Exchange rate margin: 2–4% from mid-market rate
- No fee for recipient

**Timing:**
- Cash pickup: Minutes to 24 hours
- Bank transfer: 1–3 business days

**Wise (formerly TransferWise):**
Transfers to Indonesian bank accounts. Requires having or opening a local account, which is impractical for most tourists.

## Airport Currency Exchange

**Upon Arrival:**
Airports have currency exchange counters for immediate cash needs.

**Pricing:**
- Exchange rates: 10–15% worse than city money changers
- Convenience premium for immediate needs
- Best for small amounts only (Rp1.000.000–2.000.000)

**Recommendation:**
Exchange only what you need for transport and first day. Use city money changers or ATMs for larger amounts.

## Comparing the Options

| Method | Best For | Rate Quality | Convenience | Fees |
|--------|----------|--------------|-------------|------|
| Authorized money changer | Large amounts | Excellent | Moderate | None |
| BCA/Mandiri/BNI ATM | Regular access | Very Good | High | Home bank only |
| Other bank ATM | When no-fee banks unavailable | Very Good | High | Rp30.000–75.000 + home bank |
| Hotel exchange | Emergencies only | Poor | Very High | 5–10% margin |
| Airport exchange | Immediate arrival needs | Poor | Very High | 10–15% margin |
| Western Union | Emergency, no cards working | Fair | Moderate | $5–50 sender fee |
| Prepaid travel card | Multi-country trips | Good | High | Setup required |

## Practical Recommendations by Situation

**For Short Trips (1–2 weeks):**
- Bring $200–500 cash in major currency (USD/EUR)
- Use BCA/Mandiri/BNI ATMs for additional needs
- Avoid opening new accounts or complex setups

**For Longer Trips (3+ weeks):**
- Combination of ATM withdrawals and cash exchange
- Consider prepaid travel card for better rate tracking
- Establish pattern with one ATM bank for consistency

**For Remote Area Travel:**
- Secure cash before leaving cities
- Limited ATM access outside major towns
- Carry more cash than urban travel

**For Business Travelers:**
- Company credit cards widely accepted at hotels/restaurants
- Cash for incidentals and small vendors
- Receipt retention for expense reports

## What You Need to Exchange Money or Use ATMs

**For Money Changers:**
- Valid passport (required for amounts over Rp50.000.000)
- Foreign currency cash in acceptable condition
- Patience to count and verify received rupiah

**For ATMs:**
- PIN-enabled debit or credit card
- 4-digit PIN (some ATMs have issues with longer PINs)
- Card on Visa, Mastercard, Cirrus, or Maestro network
- Awareness of your home bank's daily limits

**For All Methods:**
- Understanding of current exchange rates (check XE or similar)
- Backup payment method
- Emergency contact information for card issuers

## Red Flags to Avoid

**When Exchanging Cash:**
- Rates significantly better than mid-market (usually bait for scams)
- No visible license or authorization
- Pressure to complete transaction quickly
- Refusal to provide receipt

**When Using ATMs:**
- Standalone machines in isolated locations
- Machines with visible tampering or attachments
- Private ATMs not affiliated with major banks
- Requests for PIN from anyone

## Key Takeaways

1. **Authorized money changers offer best rates** for cash exchanges
2. **BCA, Mandiri, and BNI ATMs** have no local withdrawal fees
3. **Bring clean, undamaged bills** for exchange—torn or marked notes may be rejected
4. **Airport and hotel exchanges** are convenient but cost 10–15% more
5. **ATMs use interbank rates** within 1–2% of published rates
6. **Home bank fees** (1–3%) apply to most ATM transactions
7. **Credit card cash advances** are expensive and best avoided
8. **Western Union** works for emergencies but has transfer fees
9. **Multiple methods** provide redundancy if one fails
10. **Keep exchange receipts** until departure in case of disputes
